Actual structure of the Aanvikshiki website
1. Hero / Brand Philosophy

The first section establishes the intellectual positioning of the company.

Main messaging revolves around:

“Intelligence Amplified. Ethics Applied.”

The hero is not product-focused.
It’s mission-focused.

Core themes:

AI guided by ethics

strategic thinking

human judgment

applied intelligence

Purpose of this section:

Establish intellectual credibility
+
Frame the philosophy behind the company

So instead of saying “we build AI tools”, the site starts with why the company exists.

2. The Problem Framing Section

Next the page describes the modern problems organizations face.

Themes include:

Data chaos and fragmentation

Information overload

difficulty applying research in practice

organizations struggling to adopt AI effectively

These problems frame the company as a bridge between knowledge and execution.

Conceptually it is:

Complex world
+
too much information
+
not enough real implementation

This is classic consulting positioning.

3. Capability / Solution Framework

After explaining the problems, the site introduces the solution philosophy.

This section talks about:

AI enabled strategy

research-driven innovation

systems thinking

human + machine intelligence

This is where the consulting capability becomes visible.

The structure looks like:

Problem → fragmented knowledge

Solution → applied AI systems

But the tone remains philosophical rather than technical.

4. Products / Initiatives

The page then introduces projects or products connected to the ecosystem.

One example is EaseCare, a care coordination platform.

EaseCare focuses on improving access to care services and coordination between caregivers and families.

The role of this section is important:

It proves that the company does not only consult.

They also:

build products
run initiatives
apply research

This builds credibility.

5. Thought Leadership / Research Tone

Another major theme of the page is knowledge creation.

The messaging suggests:

AI should augment human intelligence

research must translate to real impact

ethical thinking should guide technology

This part of the site positions the company almost like a think-tank + engineering studio.

6. Call To Action

Finally the site ends with:

collaboration

partnerships

conversations

Typical messaging here:

Work with us
Collaborate
Explore solutions

This is the consulting conversion layer.

So the site’s real structure is this

Not the visual layout.
The strategic narrative.

1 Philosophy / Mission
2 Problem Framing
3 Capability Framework
4 Products / Initiatives
5 Thought Leadership
6 Collaboration CTA

That’s the backbone.

The interesting thing about this website

The structure is closer to a think-tank or research institute than a normal SaaS company.

Compare:

Typical SaaS site:

Hero
Features
Pricing
Testimonials
Demo

Aanvikshiki site:

Philosophy
Problems
Intelligence frameworks
Products
Collaboration

So the vibe is more like:

AI studio
+
research lab
+
consulting firm
The problem with the current structure (honest critique)

From a product design perspective, the site has a weakness.

It is too abstract.

Visitors may struggle to quickly understand:

What exactly the company builds
Who should hire them
What services they offer

The philosophy is strong.

But the clarity of offerings is weaker.

Which is why your new landing page should combine this philosophy with SaaS clarity.

What your improved structure should be

Take the philosophy of Aanvikshiki but combine it with modern B2B UX clarity.

Your structure should become:

1 Hero (mission + what you do)
2 Credibility / capabilities
3 Services
4 Product showcase (EaseCare)
5 Industries
6 Philosophy
7 Case studies
8 CTA

This is exactly the hybrid of Sana + Sarvam you were aiming for.

The real opportunity here

Your current site has good intellectual positioning but weak product clarity.

A modern redesign should achieve:

Philosophy
+
Product clarity
+
Service credibility

That combination is how serious AI consulting firms design their websites.
